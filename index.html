Róisín Murphy

Róisín Murphy by Flickr user Fionn Kidney

Musically adventurous, stylistically unique, and vocally smooth, Irish-born Róisín Murphy defined the 90s electronic scene, first with the group Moloko then as a solo artist. Backed by a group of jazz-inspired musicians and an army of electronic music’s top remixers, such as François K, Matthew Herbert, and Boris Dlugosch, Murphy consistently released the kind of electronic music that was both unexpected and experimental. In a nutshell, Róisín Murphy was the Radiohead of electronica, not to mention the one who paved the way for people like Lady Gaga and Adele.
